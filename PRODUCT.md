# Product

## Register

product

## Users
This app is used by a single primary user: you, logging workouts on a phone during training. The usage context is active and time-sensitive, often between sets or while moving through a session, so the interface needs to be legible at a glance, thumb-friendly, and fast to operate with minimal hesitation.

## Product Purpose
This product exists to make workout logging frictionless during training. Its job is to help you start a session quickly, log exercises and sets with minimal taps, and review past workouts without digging through clutter. Success means the app supports the workout instead of interrupting it, with a UX flow that feels direct, functional, and calm.

## Brand Personality
Focused, clean, calm. The product should feel composed and useful rather than promotional or energetic. It should reduce visual noise, keep attention on the current task, and create trust through clarity and restraint.

## Anti-references
This should not feel like a gamified fitness app. It should avoid loud or excessive color, decorative rewards, or flashy motivational patterns that distract from logging. It should also avoid heavy dashboard complexity, glossy startup-style polish that prioritizes appearance over speed, and interaction flows that force too many taps or clicks to complete routine actions.

## Design Principles
1. Reduce friction in-session: the shortest path to logging the next meaningful action should win.
2. Use color functionally: color should signal hierarchy, action, and state, never decoration.
3. Design for glanceability: important workout information should be scannable quickly under training conditions.
4. Keep the interface calm under load: even dense screens should feel orderly, not busy.
5. Prefer direct manipulation over detours: keep key actions inline and avoid unnecessary navigation or modal-heavy flows.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA contrast and interaction standards. Prioritize large thumb-friendly tap targets, strong readability in mixed or outdoor lighting conditions, and restrained motion that does not rely on animation for comprehension. The interface should remain usable when attention is split or the user is physically fatigued.
